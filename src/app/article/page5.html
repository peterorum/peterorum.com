Etching

The series of etchings was a cohesive project by virtue of the topic, medium and process. The project was loosely defined as something autobiographical, and we were given two small 20x25cm zinc plates, which were used for the entire course. Images were etched, and then almost completely scraped off as best as possible, with new drawings and techniques applied over the top throughout the course.

The autobiographical aspect I chose to describe was aspects of my daily life. On the original plates, called My Day (Figure 16), the left-hand image was based on using public transport (continuing the theme of my earlier ticket series), and journeying either to work on computers or to work on an easel. The right-hand image is based on a page from my work notebook, comprising scraps of programming, maths, doodles, phone messages and designs. The next generation, Ticket to Ryde (Figure 17), had ticket motifs etched over the top. After a lot of burnishing to try and remove most of these images from the plates, new drawings were overlain consisting of references to web page design, including standard buttons like Home and Print, cursor arrows, scrollbars, along with some of the underlying html code, e.g. <body> tags, producing Home Page (figure 18).

A separate small etching was made of a numeric key pad.
